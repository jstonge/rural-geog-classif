We adopted an integrated research approach to evaluate the social-ecological impacts of concurrent reservoir inundation from the Three Gorges Project (TGP) and national reforestation policies on farmland supply, carrying capacity, resettlement, and alternative employment. Focusing on a case study of Baota and Dongcun townships in Yunyang County, Chongqing, we conducted an in-depth assessment of every farmland patch across a study area of 80.9 $\text{km}^2$. Our methodology combined quantitative spatial analysis with qualitative social data; specifically, we utilized high-resolution QuickBird satellite imagery, thematic maps, and geographic information system (GIS) techniques to generate digital maps of land use and farmland quality. To capture the human dimension of these land-cover changes, we integrated field assessments, interviews with officials, and questionnaire surveys targeting local villagers. By synthesizing these data sources, we were able to compute farmland quality ratings and model the combined effects of "development" (reservoir construction) and "conservation" (defarming) on rural communities. This comprehensive package of methods allowed us to analyze the interaction between the loss of fertile riverside lands and the mandated reforestation of steep-slope farmlands within the specific geographical time-space regime of the Three Gorges region.
