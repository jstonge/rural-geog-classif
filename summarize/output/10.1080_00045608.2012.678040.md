# 10.1080_00045608.2012.678040

We employ a three-step methodology to analyze the factors and trends shaping cattle ranching development and its implications for deforestation in the Brazilian Legal Amazon. First, we formulate an integrated analytical framework based on a systematic literature review and qualitative data from semistructured interviews conducted with landholders and industry managers across several states, including Pará, Acre, Roraima, and Amazonas. This framework links broad policy and market drivers to specific production dynamics and subsequent land-use outcomes. Second, we utilize secondary statistical data to identify primary trends in production and trade. This includes data from FAOSTAT and the Brazilian ministries of Industry and Trade (MDIC) and Agriculture (MAPA), as well as Federal Inspection Service (SIF) records to track investments in processing facilities. We further analyze municipal-level data from the 1995-1996 and 2006 IBGE agricultural censuses to evaluate changes in herd densities, production systems, and land tenure. Finally, we link these socioeconomic trends with observed deforestation patterns by comparing census data with remote sensing analysis provided by the National Institute of Spatial Research (INPE). This integrated approach allows us to disentangle the differentiated effects of market integration and policy shifts on cattle production and landscape change across the region.
