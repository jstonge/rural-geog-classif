# 10.1080_00045608.2012.720234

We employ a livelihood approach, drawing on geography and related fields, to assess the impacts of argan oil trade on rural poverty in southwestern Morocco. By utilizing this framework, we aim to integrate the study of a biological resource-based niche commodity (BNC) into the complex web of activities through which rural households earn a living, thereby contextualizing the commodity chain. Our methodology involves testing the claim that the commercialization of argan oil—a high-value product endemic to the region—serves as an effective tool for poverty alleviation. We analyze the contribution of argan oil production to household budgets and asset accumulation, comparing its influence against other key variables such as nonfarm work and remittances. This approach allows us to evaluate how agro-ecological conditions, resource scarcity, ownership regimes, and value chain characteristics influence the actual socio-economic outcomes for producers in the Souss region.
