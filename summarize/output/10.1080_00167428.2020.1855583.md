# 10.1080_00167428.2020.1855583

We utilize geospatial data for 816,000 parcels provided by the Wisconsin State Cartographer's Office (WSCO) to analyze the extent and value of non-resident property ownership in Wisconsin's Northwoods. Our study area encompasses twenty-one counties in the northern third of the state, covering approximately 21,000 square miles. Using Version 3 data from the WSCO parcel-fabric database—which includes attributes such as owner mailing addresses, assessed fair-market values, and property tax totals—we employ the parcel tax mailing address as a proxy for primary or secondary property status. To process this data, we used Excel functions and the column-wizard tool to separate address fields, followed by geocoding via ESRI's Business Analyst Extension. 

Our methodological approach is twofold. First, we use ArcMap GIS geoprocessing tools (clip, spatial join) to identify regional patterns of non-resident land ownership and calculate summary statistics for parcel size and assessed value. Second, we conduct a localized economic analysis of four specific school districts—Grantsburg, Hayward, Hurley, and Northland Pines—to determine the impact of non-resident property taxes on public-school funding. By categorizing owners into local, non-local Wisconsin residents, and out-of-state owners, we examine how varying concentrations of tourism amenities influence capital accumulation and local institutional sustainability.
