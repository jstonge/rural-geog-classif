# 10.1080_00167428.2020.1871298

To construct alternative scenarios for rural Croatia in 2030, we integrated quantitative and qualitative approaches, acknowledging the country's postsocialist transition and EU accession as the primary framework. Our methodological design combined factor and cluster analysis to establish a rural typology, alongside Monte Carlo simulations to model potential outcomes. To incorporate expert knowledge and refine these projections, we employed the Delphi method, conducting two rounds of written questionnaires with 37 rural experts. This mixed-methods approach allowed us to synthesize complex socioeconomic variables—including demographic decline, unemployment levels, and the impacts of the Common Agricultural Policy (CAP)—across the Croatian countryside. By weaving together these statistical tools and expert consensus, we developed four distinct scenarios: Rural Renaissance, Shift, Road to Nowhere, and Growth without Development. These scenarios serve as documented assumptions to explore possible future paths for the Croatian rural landscape and to facilitate a broader discussion on neoendogenous rural development paradigms.
