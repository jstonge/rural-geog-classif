# 10.1080_00167428.2021.1890996

To document the apparent loss of sense of local heritage among inhabitants of the Belgian countryside, we employed a mixed-methods approach designed to support the reflections of the NGO 'Qualité Village Wallonie' (QVW). We first conducted seven semi-structured interviews and workshops with QVW staff and administrators, leveraging their multi-decade experience to trace longitudinal changes in community interactions with local heritage. These qualitative insights were then used in conjunction with an e-survey administered via 'framaforms' between March and June 2020. The survey, which garnered 1,091 responses from across Wallonia, utilized a short, playful questionnaire to encourage participation and minimize abandonment. We collected socio-demographic data—including age, gender, housing ownership, and duration of residence—and employed 18 Likert-scale items to measure perceived heritage richness, personal involvement, and the perceived contribution of heritage to local life. Participants were also asked to identify emblematic local places and heritage elements. This methodological framework allowed us to position individuals within a conceptual triangular model of heritage—defined by the dimensions of attachment, resource, and charge—to analyze how the perception of rural heritage is evolving in a globalized, suburbanized context.
