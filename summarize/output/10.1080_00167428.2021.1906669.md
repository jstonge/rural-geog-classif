# 10.1080_00167428.2021.1906669

We employ a life-stage approach to decompose spatiotemporal population changes along an urban-rural gradient in continental Spain from 2002 to 2017. Utilizing longitudinal demographic data from the Spanish Continuous Municipal Register (INE), we analyze population dynamics at a municipal scale across 7,914 municipalities. To capture the nuances of the urban-rural gradient, we classify municipalities into seven distinct classes based on a combination of demographic size—categorized as urban (>30,000 inhabitants), large, medium, or small—and their GIS-calculated distance from urban centers, distinguishing between suburban (<20 km) and rural (>20 km) areas.

Our analysis focuses on the absolute population and three specific birth cohorts defined by their life-course stages during the study period: a young, working-age cohort (born 1973–1992), a mature working-age cohort (born 1953–1972), and a retirement cohort (born 1943–1952). We calculate the number of inhabitants and the percentage of population variation for each group across the seven municipality classes. Furthermore, we establish population change typologies by analyzing the number and percentage of municipalities experiencing increases or decreases in both absolute population and within each of the three study cohorts.
