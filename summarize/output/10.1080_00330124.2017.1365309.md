# 10.1080_00330124.2017.1365309

Drawing on the Vermont Roots Migration Project, we employed a mixed-methods approach to explore the residential decision-making processes of rural stayers. We utilized an online anonymous survey distributed via social media networks to capture the narratives of individuals who spent at least a portion of their youth in Vermont. The survey gathered demographic data—including gender, education, and occupation—and required respondents to select from a list of reasons for staying while providing open-text narrative explanations for their residential choices. From an initial pool of 3,692 responses, we focused on a data set of 3,360 individuals aged 25 and older, specifically isolating "contented stayers"—those who reported enjoying life in Vermont and had not lived outside the state for more than one year (excluding schooling or military service). Our analysis proceeded in two stages: first, we conducted a statistical analysis using SPSS to perform crosstab analyses and chi-square tests on migration factors across groups of contentment, gender, and educational attainment. Second, we adopted a biographical approach, identifying specific respondent profiles and analyzing their open-text narratives to construct biographical sketches. This methodology allowed us to join structural patterns with individual life experiences to uncover the emotional and place-based dimensions of staying.
