# 10.1080_00330124.2019.1709214

To analyze the sociolinguistic consequences of economic convergence in Galicia, Spain, I employ a multidisciplinary approach that integrates policy analysis, historical economic review, and remote sensing. I examine the aims and application of European Union (EU) regional development and cultural policies to identify potential mismatches between economic cohesion goals and minority cultural protection. This is complemented by a historical and contemporary analysis of Galicia’s economic and sociolinguistic geographies, focusing on the region's transition from a rural agrarian society to an industrialized, technology-based economy. To quantify the physical manifestations of this shift, I utilize remote sensing tools to track land-use changes over a thirty-year period. Specifically, I analyze satellite imagery from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Earth Explorer from April 1984 and April 2014 to map the expansion of urban centers and the decline of agricultural land. By synthesizing these data sources, I investigate how urbanization and the resulting shift in population from the countryside to cities correlate with the language shift from the regional Galician language to the prestigious majority language of Castilian.
