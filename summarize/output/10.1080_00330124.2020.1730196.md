# 10.1080_00330124.2020.1730196

To examine the geographic distribution of divorce rates in Oman at a subnational level, we employed a combination of spatial autocorrelation analyses and spatial econometric regression models. Utilizing census data from 2010, our analysis focused on forty wilayats across eight governorates, encompassing diverse urban, suburban, and rural environments. We first utilized Moran’s I and both univariate and bivariate local indicators of spatial association (LISA) to identify spatial autocorrelation patterns and clusters of divorce rate associations. To model the causal influences of sociodemographic characteristics on marital dissolution, we implemented spatial econometric models, specifically spatial lag and spatial error models, within a geographic information systems (GIS) platform. The key variables examined included women's employment, female educational levels, urban residence, the proportion of economically inactive females, and the prevalence of polygamous marriage. By integrating these spatial modeling techniques, we sought to determine the extent to which the spatial structure of local communities and the geographic distribution of sociodemographic covariates predict family instability across Omani subnational boundaries.
