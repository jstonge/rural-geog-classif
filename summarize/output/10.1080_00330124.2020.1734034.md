# 10.1080_00330124.2020.1734034

We investigated the availability, accessibility, and utilization of antiretroviral treatment (ART) facilities across Botswana's 26 health districts to inform the rollout of the World Health Organization's Treat All Program. To evaluate availability, we calculated the spatial mean centers of hierarchically clustered ART facilities and visualized them in relation to population density and the road network. Accessibility was assessed using road network analysis in ArcGIS, where we assigned speed limits based on road type (tarred, gravel, or tracks) to estimate drive times to the nearest facility, incorporating a 60 km search tolerance and treating national parks as restrictive barriers. 

To analyze utilization, we utilized BAIS IV microdata to calculate HIV prevalence rates and combined this with 2017 Ministry of Health and Wellness patient data to derive ART uptake percentages at the district level. We employed a scatterplot and plot diagnostics in GeoDa to identify five rural districts characterized by high HIV prevalence (above the national rate of 18.5) and low ART uptake (below 80%). For these districts, we conducted a local-level analysis by calculating HIV patient deficits at specific facilities, using sex-standardized population estimates and the distribution of HIV-positive individuals between peri-urban villages and rural areas.
