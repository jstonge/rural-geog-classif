# 10.1080_24694452.2016.1194740

To examine the resilience divide between urban and rural areas of the United States, we employ a suite of spatial and statistical techniques utilizing the established Baseline Resilience Indicators for Communities (BRIC) measure. Our analysis focuses on the contiguous United States at the county scale, utilizing BRIC's composite framework which integrates variables across four dimensions: social resilience, economic resilience, community capital, and institutional resilience. To describe the relationships between rurality and disaster resilience, we utilize nonparametric rank analysis, analysis of variance (ANOVA), and logistic regression. Furthermore, we employ binary logistic regression to pinpoint the specific driving factors and characteristics of resilience in rural America as compared to metropolitan areas. This methodological approach allows us to identify the primary drivers of resilience—such as economic capital in urban centers versus community capital in rural regions—and to assess the spatial variability of these components across the rural landscape.
