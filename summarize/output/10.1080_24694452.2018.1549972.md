# 10.1080_24694452.2018.1549972

We employed the modified Berggren solution to map active-layer thickness (ALT) at a 30 $\times$ 30m resolution across the 150 $\text{km}^2$ Barrow Peninsula in northern Alaska, focusing on the contrasts between the urbanized City of Utqiaġvik and the relatively undisturbed tundra of the Barrow Environmental Observatory. As part of the Barrow Urban Heat Island Study, we installed seventy-one miniature data loggers within and surrounding the city to measure air and soil temperatures. These data were used to calculate air and soil surface temperature fields, as well as summer $n$-factors, categorized across nine urban and rural land-cover classes. To supplement these measurements, we integrated regional soil and land-cover maps for additional input data. We validated the model by comparing probed ALT measurements with predicted pixel values. This methodological approach allowed us to analyze how urban infrastructure influences the magnitude and geographic variability of ALT relative to surrounding natural tundra, testing the efficacy of the Berggren solution as a cartographic tool for urban geocryology.
