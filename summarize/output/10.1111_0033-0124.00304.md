# 10.1111_0033-0124.00304

To investigate land-use changes in a rural-urban environment over a thirty-five-year period (1958–1993), we developed a programming package within the MapInfo environment using MapBasic language to build temporal topology in a GIS database. Our study area covers 960 ha in the municipality of St-Eustache, Quebec. We constructed a georeferenced spatiotemporal database by producing a series of eight land-use snapshots from aerial photographs (scales 1:10,000 to 1:35,000) at approximately five-year intervals, which were then integrated with digitized cadastral and zoning maps. 

To move beyond the limitations of the snapshot model, we created a space-time composite map by superimposing subsequent land-use maps onto a 1958 template, decomposing the area into polygons with distinct attribute histories. We implemented concepts of version and mutation to establish temporal topology, utilizing variables such as type, beginning, end, duration, and uncertainty markers (beginning min, end max, and duration max) to characterize each object's evolution. This approach allowed us to perform complex spatiotemporal queries using temporal and logical operators to extract the rate of change, land-use succession patterns, and the relationship between land-use transitions and zoning regulations.
