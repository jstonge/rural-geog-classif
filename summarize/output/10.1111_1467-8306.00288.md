# 10.1111_1467-8306.00288

We evaluate the utility of radiance-calibrated Defense Meteorological Satellite Program (DMSP)-Operational Linescan System (OLS) nighttime images for extracting urban indicators in China. Using a composite of cloud-free images acquired between March 1996 and February 1997 in 30-arc-second grids, we focus on thirty-five major cities, ensuring representation from each province. With the aid of image-processing and ArcView GIS software, we reproject the data into a Lambert conic conformal projection and employ the 3-D Analysis extension to create Triangulated Irregular Network (TIN) models for each city. By treating digital numbers (DN) as altitudes, we extract two key variables from these "illuminated urban area" (IUA) domes: surface area and volume. These variables are then used as independent variables in allometric growth models to estimate nonagricultural population, gross domestic product (GDP), built-up area, and electricity consumption. We validate these estimates against 1997 data from the Statistical Bureau of China. Our approach allows us to assess the correlation between light intensity and socioeconomic indicators while examining the capacity of low-resolution nighttime imagery to reveal city morphology and the phenomenon of extended metropolitan regions.
