# 10.1111_gere.12321

We conducted this study in the Barcelona Metropolitan Region (BMR), a polycentric area in northeast Spain characterized by a mix of dense urban cores and low-density rural environments. To identify territorial disparities in youth mobility, we analyzed data from the 2014 Workday Mobility Survey (EMEF'14), which utilized Computer Assisted Telephone Interviews (CATI) with 9,461 residents. We focused our analysis on a subsample of 1,063 young adults aged eighteen to twenty-nine years, comparing their everyday mobility patterns against a reference population of older adults aged thirty to sixty-four. 

To examine the effect of territorial configuration, we categorized municipalities as either rural (under 10,000 inhabitants) or urban (over 50,000 inhabitants). We adopted a comprehensive perspective by analyzing all trip purposes—distinguishing between occupational (work/study) and personal trips—and all transportation modes, including active, private, and public transit. Key variables included trip engagement, modal split, and daily travel time expenditures. We employed cross-tabulation with Chi-squared tests and t-tests to determine significant differences in average behavior between age groups and territorial categories, performing all statistical analyses using IBM SPSS v21.
