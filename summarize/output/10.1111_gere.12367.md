# 10.1111_gere.12367

We investigate the development trajectories and organizational structures of Taobao villages in Zhejiang Province, China, to better understand the intersection of e-commerce and regional development. Our methodological approach combines quantitative data from AliResearch reports with primary empirical data collected through field research. In July 2016, we conducted surveys of online enterprises across 22 Taobao villages and industrial parks located in the cities of Hangzhou, Yiwu, and Lishui. Of the 269 survey forms personally delivered to online enterprises, we received 254 effective returns, yielding a return rate of 94.42 percent. To complement these surveys, we conducted in-depth interviews with a diverse range of local actors, including entrepreneurs, representatives from e-commerce trade associations, and village government officers. By synthesizing these data sources, we examine the roles of local states, ICT firms, and spatial networking in shaping the restructuring of rural development models. This mixed-methods approach allows us to scrutinize how rural e-tailers are embedded within broader production networks and how local contexts influence the transition from traditional rural economies to e-commerce-driven regional growth.
