# 10.1111_j.0033-0124.1996.00321.x

To develop a national rural sampling frame for the HIV Cost and Services Utilization Study (HCSUS), we employed the spatial-order method as a computationally efficient alternative to traditional location-allocation models. Our study area encompassed the 48 conterminous United States, focusing specifically on 1,853 rural (non-MSA) counties and health districts. We utilized county-level AIDS caseload data for the period from January 1991 to December 1993, collected directly from state health agencies and processed using ArcInfo GIS software. 

The methodological approach utilized space-filling (Peano) curves to transform the two-dimensional x,y coordinates of county centroids into a single linear spatial-order value between 0 and 1. By treating the spatial order as a proxy for nearness, we grouped areal units consecutively to create clusters. We established three primary criteria for these clusters: the inclusion of only rural units, a capacity constraint of approximately 50 AIDS cases per cluster to ensure a viable sample size, and the maximization of geographic contiguity. This process resulted in the generation of 226 rural clusters, which served as the primary sampling units for a three-stage probability sampling design. This approach allowed us to manage a large national dataset within a short lead time while maintaining satisfactory spatial clustering.
