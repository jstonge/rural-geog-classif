# 10.1111_j.1467-8306.1995.tb01808.x

We examine the ecological and economic impacts of land stabilization techniques on loessal soils in Zhongyang County, Shanxi Province, PRC. Our research focuses on comparing the productivity of two primary stabilization methods against two types of unstabilized land: terraces constructed by human labor (*rengong titian*), terraces constructed using earth-moving equipment (*jixie titian*), flat unterraced land (*yuandi*), and unimproved slopeland (*podian*), the latter serving as a control. We conducted a field survey of 330 households across 33 villages between October 1991 and August 1992. To ensure representative data, we randomly selected four of the county's eleven townships and employed a nested random sample of approximately 84 households per township. We evaluated six primary subsistence and economic crops—corn, millet, soybeans, sunflower, white potatoes, and sorghum—which account for over 92 percent of the land sown by our sample. To analyze the data, we generated production functions for each crop to determine the specific contribution of the four land types to gross production per plot. Additionally, we utilized multiple analysis of variance (MANOVA) to determine if mean yields for these six crops differed significantly across the four land types.
