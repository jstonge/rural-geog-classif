# 10.1111_j.1931-0846.2003.tb00035.x

We conducted two in situ surveys in December 1999 to analyze local dependence on forest resources within the Shimentai Nature Reserve (SNR) in Yingde County, Guangdong Province, China. Utilizing a stratified sampling approach, we selected seven village committees for study, categorized into "near-zone" groups (those within or possessing rights to the reserve) and "far-zone" groups (those located in the Lianjiang River floodplain). Our data collection integrated four primary methods: questionnaire surveys, face-to-face interviews, organized group discussions, and in situ observations, supplemented by a review of local gazetteers and official reserve records. 

The primary questionnaire survey was administered to 297 randomly selected households (*jia*), focusing on forest product collection, income and expenditure profiles, and socioeconomic variables including ethnicity (Hakka and Yao), household size, wealth, and out-migration. To further probe historical and current use patterns, we conducted in-depth supplementary surveys with 29 high-dependence households. To minimize non-sampling errors and overcome linguistic barriers, we recruited and trained local primary school teachers as research assistants. Data were analyzed using SPSS 9.0, employing Student's t-tests to gauge distance factors, Mann-Whitney tests for ethnic variables, and one-way analysis of variance to evaluate the influence of wealth, household size, and migrant labor on resource utilization.
