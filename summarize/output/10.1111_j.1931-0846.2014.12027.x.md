# 10.1111_j.1931-0846.2014.12027.x

We employed a qualitative case-study approach to examine the migration patterns of former smallholders to peri-urban interface (PUI) zones in western Pará state, Brazil, specifically focusing on communities near the city of Santarém. Our methodology integrated a suite of qualitative tools, including ethnographically oriented household surveys, over sixty hours of semistructured interviews, and four weeks of participant observation. We utilized purposive "snowball" sampling to recruit a diverse participant pool that included both *caboclos* and *colonos*, as well as migrants and nonmigrants, smallholder subsistence farmers, and midsized commercial farmers. 

The study was conducted across three specific sites within the municipalities of Santarém and Belterra: the town of Alter do Chão (specifically the neighborhoods of União and Nova União) and the communities of São Jorge and Piquiatuba, the latter two located within the Tapajós National Forest (FLONA). By analyzing these sites, we sought to identify the push and pull factors influencing the decision to resettle in PUI zones rather than returning to rural origins or remaining in urban centers. Key variables under consideration included access to infrastructure (education and healthcare), the availability of wage labor, the ability to maintain subsistence activities, and the impact of federal conditional cash-transfer programs on livelihood stability.
