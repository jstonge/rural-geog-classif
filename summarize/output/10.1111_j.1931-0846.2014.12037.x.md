# 10.1111_j.1931-0846.2014.12037.x

We employ a primarily descriptive research design to investigate the role of urban-rural remittances in fostering self-reliance and development within Shubbola, a rural community in northern Kordofan State, western Sudan. Our methodology integrates qualitative and quantitative approaches, utilizing in-depth interviews, focus-group discussions, and direct observation, supplemented by a review of secondary data. Fieldwork was conducted between January 2008 and January 2011. We utilized a systematic sampling technique along a central street transect to select seventy male heads of households for semi-structured interviews, focusing on variables such as remittance receipt, crop types, farm size fluctuations over the previous decade, and the adoption of tractor technology. To contextualize these findings and assess Shubbola's influence on its hinterland, we conducted two focus-group discussions: one with ten farmers from adjacent villages at the weekly market and another with local youth at the village evening club. Data analysis involved basic descriptive statistics—specifically frequency distributions and averages—processed via IBM SPSS, while qualitative data from key informants and observations were used to establish associations between remittance flows and socioeconomic transformations.
