# 10.1111_j.1931-0846.2015.12078.x

We utilize data from the National Research on Education in Agrarian Reform (*Pesquisa Nacional de Educação na Reforma Agrária* - PNERA), the only available settlement survey of its kind, to explore the success of land-reform settlements in improving living standards and providing educational access for the rural poor. Conducted in 2004 by the Ministry of Education in collaboration with INCRA and INEP, the PNERA dataset provides a comprehensive snapshot of federal land-reform settlements legalized since 1985. Our methodological approach involves a comparative analysis between the Landless Rural Workers Movement (*Movimento dos Trabalhadores Rurais Sem-Terra* - MST) and other smaller, more fragmented settlement movements. 

The PNERA data is derived from three distinct survey groups: school representatives (teachers and principals) from 8,679 schools; community leaders from 5,595 settlements; and a representative sample of 10,220 households across 510 randomly selected settlements per state. By applying sample weights, we analyze key variables including racial distribution, age, gender, and self-perceived social status, alongside indicators of residential environment and material possessions. This allows us to assess the internal mechanisms of the MST—specifically its bottom-up organizational structure and commitment to participatory education—relative to the broader landscape of Brazilian agrarian reform.
